# Perch Reader

## Design Context

### Users
A single reader (the project owner) who finds an article they want to consume but would rather listen than read — while making coffee, walking, or resting their eyes. They paste a link or raw text, press play, and follow along. Sessions are 5–30 minutes. Privacy-conscious: speech synthesis must run fully on-device.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, bookish, warm-mechanical. Like a small wooden radio in a reading nook: handcrafted, calm, but unmistakably a machine doing fine work. Emotional goals: calm focus, trust, a touch of literary charm. Never: techy, neon, dashboard-like, "AI product" aesthetics.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm reading den, light theme. Paper-warm surfaces (OKLCH warm-tinted neutrals, hue ~80–85), ink-brown text, burnt-sienna accent used sparingly (the 10%). Display face: Young Serif (warm, chunky, bookish). Body: Literata (designed for long-form screen reading). Mono: Spline Sans Mono for timecodes/status only. Text is the hero; the player is a compact dock. The sentence currently being read carries a soft marker-wash highlight that moves through the text.

Anti-references: podcast app dashboards, dark glassy players, gradient text, cyan/purple AI palettes, card grids.

### Design Principles
1. The article is the interface — typography first, chrome minimal.
2. One accent, used rarely: play control, current-sentence marker, links.
3. Every wait is narrated plainly (model download, synthesis) in calm microcopy, mono type.
4. Follow-along is sacred: the highlighted sentence and auto-scroll must always feel correct.
5. On-device pride: the UI quietly states that nothing leaves the machine.
